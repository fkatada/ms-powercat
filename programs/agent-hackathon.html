Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Copilot Studio Agent Build-A-Thon? The Agent Build-A-Thon is an event hosted by the Power CAT Team. It invites organizational teams to learn, build & showcase their agents for their real business use-cases. The high-level (tentative) agenda for this event is as follows:

- Week 1: Up to 8 hours of instructor-led hands-on labs & use case envisioning
- Weeks 2 & 3: Teams will work on building their agents during this week. They will be supported by technical experts through daily office hours and 1:1 session with the experts upon request.
- Week 4: Showcase & Feedback session to demo your creations to other participants & technical advisors and obtain valuable real-time feedback from the experts.
When is the event taking place? Power CAT will host an Agent Build-A-Thon every month starting September 2025. Registration is now open.
Who can participate? Teams from any small, medium or large size organizations may participate. Multiple teams from within an organization can also participate by submitting different use-cases. One key requirement to participate in this Agent Build-A-Thon is that you have access to Microsoft Copilot Studio in your work tenant and have any required licenses or message packs to be able to experiment in your Dev tenant.
Why should I participate in the Build-A-Thon? There are several benefits to participating in this hackathon:

- Opportunity to learn as a team
- Opportunity to build & experiment with AI Agents
- Opportunity to get direct guidance from Copilot Studio experts
- Potential Opportunity to be Featured in a Customer Story for your team & your project
- Earn a Credly badge for successful completion of your agentic prototype during the Agent Build-A-Thon.
Who can join my team? Are there any team size restrictions? All team members should be employed by the same organization. A project team can range from 2 to 5 members.
What are the requirements for the Build-A-Thon project? Your project must include building an Agent using Copilot Studio. It may be a conversational or an autonomous agent. It should, preferably, use Gen-AI orchestration. It should address a real business need and demonstrate one or more compliance aspects of the agent for enterprise readiness.
Where do I build my project? You are required to build your project within your enterprise tenant and approved environment and ensure you have the necessary licenses and credits
How do I showcase my project? During week 4 of the Agent Build-A-Thon, we will host a Showcase & Feedback session, where your team will have the opportunity to showcase the agent you have built, its use case, and impact in front of the experts & other participants for real-time feedback.
Who can I contact for more information? For any additional questions about the Agent Build-A-Thon, please reach out to Agent BAT Support. For technical project-related questions, technical SMEs will be available during the Build-A-Thon days through a dedicated Teams channel.
